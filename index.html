Warhammer 40k Miniature Painting Guide

Welcome to the ultimate guide for painting Warhammer 40k miniatures! Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned hobbyist, this page will help you explore different techniques to bring your miniatures to life. Dive into the list below to discover various painting styles and methods.

  • Base Coating

    Start by applying a base coat to your miniature. This is the foundation layer that ensures the colors you apply later are vibrant and consistent.

  • Dry Brushing

    Dry brushing is a technique used to highlight raised areas of a miniature. Use a dry brush with a small amount of paint to lightly brush over the edges and details.

  • Washing

    Washes are thin, watery paints that flow into the recesses of a miniature, creating shadows and depth. Apply a wash after base coating to enhance the details.

  • Layering

    Layering involves applying multiple thin layers of paint to build up color and create smooth transitions. This technique is great for achieving realistic effects.

  • Edge Highlighting

    Edge highlighting is the process of painting thin lines along the edges of a miniature to make the details pop. Use a lighter shade of the base color for this technique.

  • Weathering

    Weathering adds a worn, battle-damaged look to your miniatures. Techniques include chipping, rust effects, and mud splatters.

  • Object Source Lighting (OSL)

    OSL simulates the effect of light coming from a specific source on the miniature, such as a glowing weapon or lantern. This advanced technique requires careful blending and shading.
